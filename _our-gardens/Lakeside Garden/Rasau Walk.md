<p>Situated along the edge of Jurong Lake, Rasau Walk is a 300m boardwalk
that meanders through clusters of Nibong (<em><a rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Oncosperma&nbsp;</a>tigillarium</em>),
Sealing Wax Palm (<em>Cyrtostachys renda</em>), and Rasau (<em>Pandanus spp.</em>).</p>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Lakeside Garden/Rasau Walk/Rasau_Walk_2.jpg">
</div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>A restored freshwater swamp, Rasau Walk contains over 50 species of plants,
which are found in naturally occurring freshwater swamp forests.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Lakeside Garden/Rasau Walk/Rasau_Walk_3.jpg">
</div>
<p>The interface between land and water also provides feeding opportunities
for a large variety of shore birds, such as the Grey Heron (<em>Ardea cinerea</em>)
and White-breasted Waterhen (<em>Amaurornis phoenicurus</em>).</p>
